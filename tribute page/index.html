Mother Teresa

Saint Teresa of Calcutta

Who was Mother Teresa?

Mother Teresa, born Agnes Gonxha Bojaxhiu on August 26, 1910, was a Catholic nun who devoted her life to serving the poor and sick. She founded the Missionaries of Charity in 1950, a religious congregation dedicated to serving the poorest of the poor in India and other parts of the world.

Her work and achievements

During her lifetime, Mother Teresa and her sisters cared for thousands of people in need, including the sick, the dying, and the abandoned. She was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 1979 for her humanitarian work and was canonized as a saint by the Catholic Church in 2016.

Legacy

Mother Teresa's selfless dedication to serving the poorest of the poor has inspired millions of people around the world. Her legacy lives on through the Missionaries of Charity, which continues to serve the poorest of the poor in many countries.
